My Personal Strenghts



  1. Temperance- I see myself as a person who is very thoughtful and not too emotional or too detached. Wants nothing more than to make themselves better.Can manage to be soft but also to be hard. I try to be the best of myself even in the worst of situations.I try to be forgiving and understanding in most situations even tho it is mostly taken advantage of.

  2. Wisdom- I remind myself that i am the one that dertermins my own reality, I am able to entertain other persons thoughts or views without being persuaded into thinking that it's my own. A wise man once said
    A fool thinks himself to be wise, but a wise man knows himself to be a fool
    .

  3. Courage-Courage is what it takes to stand up and speak; courage is also what it takes to sit down and listen. (Winston Churchill).I practice to have Hope in most situations,I stay confident that one way or another things WILL and MUST work out.I train myself to accept the reality of most things so that I am not easily devastated.I accept that I am not in control of things because I understand that life is a series of fortunate and unfortunate events where I don't always get what I want or need.

    In chapter 5, Charlie discusses to Michael the value of his goals and the need to steer clear of regrets. Charlie clarifies the issue by contrasting the pain of regret with the pain of discipline. Then he advised Michael to make the most of each day because there is just one finest opportunity out of all the opportunities that come along. And cautioned him against making the mistake of prioritizing urgent over important tasks when managing his time. Michael was even told to establish his own life plans rather than adopting others'.

    • Every positive opportunity that shows itself in life we should try and gravitate to it. People often miss great opportunities because of shyness or because of fear, but every positive opportunity is a chance to further develop our lives.

    In Chapter 6, Charlie emphasized to Michael the value of associating with upbeat individuals rather to those who are continually whining about their circumstances and doing nothing to change it. If you want to be successful, surround yourself with successful people. When you are around your friends, family, coworkers, and other people, you should feel empowered to make any improvements to your life that you can.

    • The type of persons you hang around can really determine the type of person you come out to be.There is a quote that saysShow me your friends and I will tell you who you are. It is always best to stick around positive and hardworking individuals because they can guide you to a bright future

    In Chapter 7, Charlie emphasized to Michael the value of lifelong learning in order to advance and thrive. Your success in life is not guaranteed by your formal education.Three methods for keeping learning active are reading books to be exposed to new ideas, ways of thinking, and cultures, watching successful individuals, and reflecting on and learning from your own experiences.

    • Everyday we live we learn new stuff. Whether by heartbreak, love, discipline or simply even having a conversation. It is always best to keep on expanding your knowlege so you can develop new skills, habits and even new traits.
